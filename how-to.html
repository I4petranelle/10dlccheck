What gets checked — and why it matters

Every check maps to a CTIA rule or active carrier enforcement pattern. Violations reduce your 10DLC brand trust score and increase the risk of message filtering.

Must Fix

Brand identity

CTIA requires businesses to identify themselves in every A2P message. Missing brand names are among the most common filtering triggers across all major US carriers.

Must Fix

STOP / opt-out keyword

All A2P campaigns must support STOP. First messages must include opt-out instructions. Campaigns without STOP support face suspension under CTIA Messaging Principles.

Must Fix

Public link shorteners

Bit.ly, tinyurl, and similar services are associated with spam and phishing campaigns. Branded or first-party domains are required to maintain delivery rates on 10DLC.

High Risk

SHAFT content

Sex, Hate, Alcohol, Firearms, and Tobacco are restricted categories. Registered campaigns still face strict filtering for SHAFT content regardless of campaign type.

Review

Financial and urgency claims

Get-rich-quick language, aggressive urgency phrases, and unlicensed financial claims match known spam patterns that activate carrier machine-learning filters.

Review

HELP keyword support

Responses to HELP must deliver business contact information. This check flags message flows where opt-in language implies ongoing communication without HELP support configured.

Must Fix

Deceptive language patterns

Fake prize claims, misleading calls-to-action, and scam-adjacent phrasing are detected by both carrier ML systems and our rule engine, causing immediate filtering.

Advisory

Message length and segments

Messages over 160 characters split into multiple billing segments. The checker counts segments in real time and warns when you approach limits that increase campaign cost.

Optional

AI clarity suggestions

Opt-in grammar and tone analysis helps make your compliant message as clear and effective as possible, returned alongside the compliance check results.
