What Is 10DLC and Why It Matters

10DLC stands for 10-Digit Long Code—a U.S. carrier program that allows businesses to send Application-to-Person (A2P) text messages from standard 10-digit numbers with verified brand and campaign registration. The goal is to reduce spam, improve trust, and increase deliverability for legitimate business messaging.

What Does 10DLC Do?

Under 10DLC, each company registers its brand (the business entity) and one or more campaigns (the purpose of messaging). Registration data flows through The Campaign Registry (TCR), which carriers use to determine throughput and trust levels.

10DLC vs Short Codes vs Toll-Free SMS

  • Short Codes: 5–6 digit numbers for very high volume; expensive but fast throughput.
  • Toll-Free SMS: 800/888 style numbers; moderate throughput and good for support or notifications.
  • 10DLC: Standard local numbers; affordable, brand-linked, and great for ongoing business texting.

Who Must Register?

Any organization sending A2P messages in the U.S.—marketing, alerts, appointment reminders, or OTP—should complete 10DLC brand and campaign registration to avoid filtering and potential fees.

Staying Compliant (Quick Checklist)

  • Identify your brand or sender in each message.
  • Include required keywords: STOP (opt-out) and HELP behavior.
  • Avoid public link shorteners (e.g., bit.ly); prefer branded domains.
  • Do not request or transmit sensitive personal data.
  • Keep under ~918 characters to avoid excessive concatenation.
