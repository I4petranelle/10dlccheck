What Is 10DLC?

An official guide to what 10DLC means for business message delivery and compliance.

On this page

    Definition & Purpose

    10DLC (10-Digit Long Code) is a U.S. messaging classification for Application-to-Person (A2P) SMS. It allows businesses to send text messages from standard 10-digit phone numbers while maintaining higher throughput, accountability, and trust.

    Major carriers such as AT&T, Verizon, and T-Mobile require 10DLC registration to reduce spam, protect users, and ensure quality delivery. Since December 1, 2024, U.S. carriers have disabled unregistered 10DLC traffic. Compliance is now mandatory.

    Registration Process

    To use 10DLC, you must complete Brand Registration and Campaign Registration with The Campaign Registry (TCR). Microsoft Docs explains how brand verification is required first, followed by campaign approval.

    • Brand registration: your company credentials (legal name, EIN, address)
    • Campaign registration: your use case, message samples, opt-in and throughput expectations

    Approval times vary — brand registration can take several business days, and campaigns may take longer if manual review is required.

    Compliance Essentials

    • Consent required: send messages only to those who explicitly opted in
    • Brand & voice identity: identify who is sending the message within the content
    • STOP / HELP keywords: support opt-out and help queries (STOP, UNSUBSCRIBE, HELP)
    • Link policy: avoid public link shorteners (like bit.ly) in first messages; carriers often penalize them
    • Content restrictions: prohibited categories include gambling, debt relief, adult content, and firearms.
    • List hygiene: remove inactive or deactivated numbers, and respect opt-outs promptly
    Carriers may apply surcharges or block non-compliant traffic. Failure to comply can lead to filtering or fines.

    Examples

    ✅ Compliant Welcome Message

    “Welcome to ShopCo deals! Msg & data rates may apply. Max 3 msgs/week. Reply STOP to unsubscribe.”

    ❌ Risky First Message

    “50% off today only! Click bit.ly/offer” — lacks sender identity, no STOP, uses a public short link.

    FAQs

    Do I need 10DLC if I already use short code or toll-free?

    No. 10DLC is ideal for local long-code messaging. Short codes and toll-free numbers remain valid with their own registration and compliance rules.

    How strict are volume limits?

    Carriers assign throughput based on your campaign’s trust score. Exceeding limits may trigger filtering or penalties.

    Which carriers enforce this?

    All major U.S. carriers — AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile (including Sprint), and others — require 10DLC registration. Carrier Code of Conduct
