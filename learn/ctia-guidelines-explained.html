CTIA Messaging Guidelines Explained (2025 Update)

Updated for 2026: Understand consent, opt-outs, identity and content rules to keep your A2P SMS compliant and deliverable.

By 10dlccheck Editorial • Published October 16, 2025 • Updated April 7, 2026 • ~3 min read

Overview

The CTIA Messaging Principles and Best Practices define how businesses should send application-to-person (A2P) messages across U.S. carrier networks. They focus on consumer consent, transparency and responsible messaging behavior.

These are industry guidelines, not laws but carriers actively enforce them alongside regulations like TCPA and FCC rules.

What changed in the 2025 CTIA update?

  • Stronger enforcement of explicit opt-in requirements
  • Mandatory brand identification in messages
  • Increased filtering for misleading or unregistered traffic
  • Closer alignment between carriers on enforcement rules

Consent

  • Obtain clear, documented opt-in before sending messages
  • Explain message purpose and frequency upfront
  • Do not use purchased or shared contact lists
“Text JOIN to 12345 to receive weekly updates. Msg & data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out.”

Identity and transparency

  • Clearly identify your brand in every message
  • Use branded domains instead of generic short links
  • Ensure phone numbers match your business identity

Opt-out and HELP support

  • Support STOP, UNSUBSCRIBE, and CANCEL
  • Respond to HELP with support info
  • Process opt-outs immediately across all campaigns

Content standards

  • Avoid deceptive, illegal, or misleading content
  • Clearly state offer terms
  • Do not use evasion tactics like traffic splitting

Numbers and provisioning

  • Only use numbers you are authorized to text-enable
  • Monitor shared numbers for abuse
  • Remove inactive numbers to reduce complaints

Mitigating filtering and blocking

  • Track opt-outs, complaints and delivery issues
  • Avoid sudden traffic spikes
  • Keep messaging aligned with your registered campaign

Examples

Compliant message

“BrandName: 20% off this week only at brand.com/sale. Reply STOP to opt out.”

Non-compliant message

“50% OFF NOW: bit.ly/xyz123”

  • No brand identification
  • Uses link shortener
  • No opt-out instructions

FAQ

What are CTIA messaging guidelines?

They are industry standards for consent, transparency and responsible SMS messaging.

Are CTIA rules legally required?

No, but carriers enforce them alongside legal regulations.

What opt-out keywords are required?

STOP, UNSUBSCRIBE and CANCEL must be supported.

Should I use branded links?

Yes, branded domains improve trust and reduce filtering.
