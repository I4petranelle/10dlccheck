CTIA Guidelines Explained

A Guide to CTIA Messaging Principles & Best Practices (May 2023)

1. Introduction & Purpose

The CTIA Messaging Principles and Best Practices provide a voluntary framework to encourage trusted wireless messaging, protect consumers, and clarify expectations for all participants in the messaging ecosystem.

  • Encourage trusted and effective wireless messaging (SMS, MMS, RCS)
  • Protect consumers from unwanted or fraudulent content
  • Provide clarity to senders, providers, and carriers
  • Complement (but not replace) legal obligations under TCPA, CAN-SPAM, FCC rules

These guidelines primarily cover messaging using 10-digit long codes (10DLC) and short codes. They apply to message senders, aggregators, carriers, and service providers operating within the U.S. wireless ecosystem.

2. Key Concepts & Roles

The CTIA distinguishes between Consumer (P2P) and Non-Consumer (A2P) messaging. Business and organizational messaging is considered Non-Consumer and held to stricter consent, content, and privacy standards.

Consumer Messaging

Messaging between individuals — conversational, lower volume, and balanced send/receive ratios.

Non-Consumer Messaging

Business or organizational messaging to consumers — such as marketing, alerts, or notifications. These require clear consent, opt-outs, and transparency.

3. Non-Consumer Best Practices

3.1 Consumer Consent

Consent is the foundation of compliance. Senders must obtain, document, and honor consumer opt-ins.

Example CTA: “Text JOIN123 to 55555 to receive weekly offers from YourCompany. Msg & data rates may apply. Up to 4 msgs/week. Reply STOP to cancel.”
  • Opt-ins must be explicit and campaign-specific.
  • Confirmation messages must include sender name, opt-out info, frequency, and cost.
  • Opt-outs must be simple and immediate (via STOP, UNSUBSCRIBE, CANCEL, etc.).
  • Do not rent, buy, or share consent lists.

3.2 Privacy & Security

Maintain a clear privacy policy and implement strong security controls (access, encryption, audits). Remove deactivated numbers promptly.

3.3 Content Requirements

  • No deceptive, fraudulent, or illegal content.
  • Links must identify the sender; public link shorteners are discouraged.
  • Phone numbers within messages must identify the associated entity.

3.4 Text-Enabling a Telephone Number

Senders must validate authority to use a 10-digit number for messaging. It should be properly assigned through a telecom provider.

3.5 Other Practices

  • Avoid “snowshoe” tactics (distributing traffic over many numbers to evade filters).
  • Do not use unauthorized routes (“grey routes”).
  • Monitor proxy or shared numbers closely for abuse.

4. Special Use Cases

The CTIA also provides guidance for group messaging, number spoofing, and registries — requiring fairness, transparency, and opt-out support in all implementations.

5. Mitigating Unwanted Messaging

  • Providers should proactively block or filter unwanted messages.
  • Consumers can report spam via 7726 (“SPAM”).
  • Bad actors may be suspended or blacklisted.

6. Carrier & Industry Implications

  • Carriers may filter traffic based on opt-in status, reputation, or complaint rates.
  • Non-compliant senders risk message blocking or throttling.
  • Maintain detailed opt-in/out logs for audits.

7. Example Scenarios

Promotional Campaign

“Text OFFERS to 12345 to receive deals from ShopCo. Up to 3 msgs/week. Reply STOP to unsubscribe.”

→ Send confirmation, log consent, and include STOP in all subsequent messages.

Informational Alerts

“Alert: Unusual login attempt detected. Reply YES if you recognize it.”

Ensure branding, domain transparency, and opt-out for non-essential messaging.

8. Legal Context

CTIA guidelines are voluntary, not law. Compliance with TCPA, CAN-SPAM, FCC, and state laws remains mandatory. Carriers may impose stricter internal rules.

9. Implementation Tips

  • Integrate compliance early in your workflow.
  • Use dedicated numbers to isolate risk.
  • Monitor opt-outs, bounces, and complaint rates.
  • Review your messaging strategy quarterly to align with CTIA updates.
