Breaking

Auth+ 2.0 deadline: legacy public-profit brands must verify by October 30, 2025

TCR's Authentication+ 2.0 program requires all public, for-profit brands registered before October 17, 2024 with at least one active campaign to complete identity verification by this date. New public-profit brands registering from August 7, 2025 face mandatory Auth+ vetting at registration, with a fee of $12.50 per brand. Brands that miss the deadline risk campaign suspension.

Apr 2025
11
Breaking

Opt-out requests must now be honored within 10 business days

As of April 11, 2025, senders must process STOP opt-out requests within 10 business days. This is now a formalized carrier requirement, not a best practice recommendation. Failure to honor opt-outs within this window can result in campaign suspension and escalating penalties. Senders using shared opt-out lists across multiple campaigns must track and revoke consent per campaign.

Ref: FTC CAN-SPAM →

1
Breaking

All unregistered 10DLC traffic fully blocked by US carriers, no exceptions

As of February 1, 2025, all three major US carriers block 100% of unregistered A2P long-code traffic. Messages from unregistered numbers are silently dropped or returned with an error code. There is no grace period. Any business using 10-digit numbers for automated, bulk, or scheduled SMS or MMS must have completed both Brand and Campaign registration through TCR before sending.

1
Breaking

Syniverse, the last major DCA supporting unregistered traffic, ended service on December 1, 2024

Syniverse was the last Direct Connect Aggregator still allowing unregistered 10DLC traffic to pass through. Their cutoff closed every remaining unregistered pathway, including for sole proprietors without EINs. Businesses relying on this route were required to complete TCR registration immediately. Sole Proprietor use cases require the separate SP 2.0 registration process with per-carrier rate limits and monthly reporting obligations.

Oct 2024
17
Important

TCR launches Authentication+ to reduce brand impersonation and smishing

TCR introduced Authentication+ as a one-time brand-representative identity check to combat impersonation, spoofing, and consumer-targeted fraud including smishing. At launch it applies to public, for-profit brands only. An authorized employee receives an email with a 2FA verification link. Brands owned by publicly traded parent companies should confirm whether they fall within scope.

Source: TCR →

Pricing

Carrier surcharges raised: unregistered SMS now $0.012 per message, MMS $0.021

Carriers raised per-message fees for unregistered A2P traffic. Unregistered SMS moved to $0.012 per message and MMS to $0.021 per message, significantly above registered campaign rates. These surcharges apply on top of normal messaging costs and compounded with the risk of complete blocking that followed later in the year.

2024
Important

Carriers impose fines up to $10,000 per violation for prohibited SHAFT content in SMS

Carriers actively fine senders for messages containing SHAFT content: Sex, Hate, Alcohol, Firearms, and Tobacco. Fines reach up to $10,000 per violation regardless of registration status. Violations can result in number blacklisting and account suspension in addition to monetary penalties. The CTIA Messaging Principles define SHAFT as a restricted content category for all A2P messaging.

CSPs and messaging platforms may pass these costs through at different rates. Confirm your exact amounts with your provider or directly through the TCR portal.

2023
Important

Sole Proprietor 2.0: strict per-carrier rate limits and monthly TCR reporting now required

TCR's Sole Proprietor 2.0 requirements impose per-carrier, per-campaign rate limits and mandatory monthly message reporting to TCR for each active SP campaign. Software providers enabling SP messaging must vet their SP customers, enforce carrier rate limits, submit monthly reports to TCR, and work with a CNP whose DCA supports the SP use case. That unregistered pathway is now fully closed as of December 2024.

Source: TCR →

1
Breaking

First hard enforcement date: US carriers begin blocking all unregistered 10DLC traffic

September 1, 2023 was the first industry-wide hard enforcement date for 10DLC registration. US carriers began blocking all unregistered A2P messages sent from local 10-digit numbers. Numbers not yet registered with TCR experienced immediate delivery failures. Completing both Brand and Campaign registration through TCR was required before sending could resume.

Source: TCR →
May 2023
Important

CTIA publishes updated Messaging Principles and Best Practices, replacing the 2019 edition

CTIA published its updated Messaging Principles and Best Practices in May 2023. Key changes include renaming A2P messaging as Non-Consumer messaging, expanded consent requirements including explicit opt-in per campaign, a requirement to confirm opt-in for recurring programs, stronger rules against renting or selling opt-in lists, and updated guidance on embedded links and shared short codes.

May 2023
Important

CTIA Section 5.1.2.2: one opt-in per campaign, consent cannot transfer across programs

The updated CTIA guidelines explicitly require one opt-in per campaign. Consent obtained for one messaging program cannot be applied to a different service, product, or campaign. Renting, selling, or sharing opt-in lists is prohibited. Businesses running multiple campaign types such as promotional and transactional must collect and document separate opt-ins for each.

Source: CTIA Section 5.1.2.2 →
May 2023
Important

CTIA Section 5.3.2: branded HTTPS links required, public URL shorteners flagged as high risk

CTIA Section 5.3.2 states that embedded links in SMS messages must use branded, secure HTTPS domains. Public URL shorteners including bit.ly, tinyurl.com, and goo.gl are considered high risk and are subject to carrier-level filtering. Using public shorteners is one of the most common causes of campaign filtering and blocking.

Source: CTIA Section 5.3.2 →
May 2023
FYI

CTIA Sections 5.5.2 and 5.5.3: snowshoe messaging and grey routing explicitly prohibited

The 2023 CTIA Best Practices formally prohibit snowshoe messaging, which is distributing bulk traffic across many numbers to evade filters; grey routing, which bypasses carrier networks to avoid filtering or fees; and misuse of proxy numbers. These practices can result in immediate suspension across all numbers and campaigns associated with the sender.

Source: CTIA Sections 5.5.2-5.5.3 →

FYI

FCC Enforcement Bureau lists unlawful A2P messaging, spoofing, and TCPA violations as a top priority

The FCC Enforcement Bureau lists unlawful communications including illegal robocalls, spoofed caller ID, and unsolicited commercial text messages as a top priority. The FCC coordinates with carriers to block traffic at the network level and pursues civil penalties under the TCPA and Communications Act. Senders should maintain documented consent records and ensure clear sender identification in every message.

Ongoing
FYI

FTC CAN-SPAM Act applies to commercial SMS: opt-outs must be honored promptly

The FTC CAN-SPAM Compliance Guide applies to commercial electronic messages including SMS. Key requirements include clear sender identification, honest message content, a working opt-out mechanism, and prompt honoring of unsubscribe requests. The FTC can impose civil penalties per violation. Treat CAN-SPAM as a compliance floor, not a ceiling.

Source: FTC CAN-SPAM Guide →
2024
Important

Verbal opt-in requires written confirmation in the first message with an explicit call to action

When a subscriber agrees to join a campaign verbally, such as in-store or over the phone, the business must confirm that consent in the first SMS sent. The confirmation must explain what program the consumer has joined, how often they will receive messages, and how to opt out. This must be documented alongside sample messages in TCR registration materials.

Source: TCR →
2020
FYI

The Campaign Registry (TCR) launched in 2020 as the central authority for A2P 10DLC registration

TCR was established in 2020 by major US carriers to serve as the central registry for A2P 10DLC messaging. Brands receive a Trust Score from 0 to 100 based on vetting, which determines their carrier throughput tier. CSPs submit registrations on behalf of brands, and DCAs perform additional compliance vetting before campaigns are approved for traffic.
