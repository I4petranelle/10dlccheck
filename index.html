The 2026 SMS Compliance Checklist

To maintain a high deliverability trust score, every 10DLC campaign must adhere to the following CTIA Messaging Principles:

Explicit Consent You must obtain documented "Prior Express Written Consent" before sending the first message.
Clear Identification Every message must start with your brand name.
Opt-Out Availability You must support and honor all mandatory unsubscribe keywords.
Help Accessibility Users must be able to receive support instructions via text.

Mandatory CTIA STOP & Opt-Out Keywords

Your 10DLC registry and SMS platform must recognize these five specific keywords to prevent carrier blocks: STOP, END, QUIT, CANCEL, UNSUBSCRIBE.

Compliant Opt-Out Template

[Brand Name]: You have successfully opted out. You will receive no further messages. Reply START to resubscribe.

Mandatory HELP & INFO Keywords

CTIA guidelines require a functional HELP keyword response that provides clear contact instructions.

Compliant HELP Template

[Brand Name] Support: For help, email support@yourdomain.com or call 800-XXX-XXXX. Msg&Data rates may apply. Reply STOP to unsubscribe.

Why CTIA Compliance Matters for 10DLC

The CTIA Messaging Principles serve as the framework for how Tier 1 carriers — Verizon, AT&T, and T-Mobile — filter traffic. If your campaign generates a high volume of opt-out attempts without a proper automated response, your 10DLC Brand trust score will drop. The consequences include increased per-message surcharges, reduced message throughput, and total campaign suspension without warning.
